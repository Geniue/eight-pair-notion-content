FB Ads Steps


  • Create a Facebook Ads account
    The first step is to create a Facebook Ads account. Follow the below steps to create a Facebook Ads account:
    • - Go to [Facebook Ads Manager](https://www.facebook.com/ads/manager).
    • Click on "Create Account" and follow the prompts to set up your account.
  • Connect your Instagram account
    Once you have set up your Facebook Ads account, you need to connect your Instagram account to it. Follow the below steps to connect your Instagram account:
    • Click on "Settings" in the top right corner of the Ads Manager.
    • Click on "Instagram Accounts" in the left-hand menu.
    • Click on "Add" and enter your Instagram login credentials.
  • Connect your Facebook Pixel
    The Facebook Pixel is a piece of code that you place on your website to track user behavior and conversions. Follow the below steps to connect your Facebook Pixel:
    • - Go to [Facebook Ads Manager](https://www.facebook.com/ads/manager).
    • Click on "Ads Manager" in the top left corner of the screen.
    • Click on "Pixels" in the "Measure & Report" column.
    • Click on "Create a Pixel" and follow the prompts to create your pixel.
    • Click on "Create a Pixel" and follow the prompts to create your pixel.
  • Create a retargeting campaign
    After connecting the Instagram account to Facebook Ads and connecting your Facebook Pixel, the next step is to create a retargeting campaign. Follow the below steps to create a retargeting campaign:
    • Click on "Create" in the top right corner of the Ads Manager.
    • Select "Campaign".
    • Choose your campaign objective. The campaign objective should be aligned with your marketing goals, such as increasing brand awareness, generating leads, or driving sales.
    • Name your campaign. Make sure the name is descriptive and makes it easy to identify the campaign.
    • Set your campaign budget and schedule. The budget should be set based on your retargeting audience size and campaign goals. The schedule should be set based on the time when your audience is most active.
    • Choose your ad set settings. An ad set is a group of ads that share the same target audience, budget, and schedule. Choose your ad set settings based on your retargeting audience segments.
    • Select "Create Ad".
  • Create retargeting ads for Instagram profile engagers
    After creating a retargeting campaign, the next step is to create retargeting ads specifically for users who have engaged with your Instagram profile. Follow the below steps to create retargeting ads for Instagram profile engagers:
    • Choose your ad format. The ad format should be aligned with your campaign objective and the ad set settings.
    • Choose your ad creative. The ad creative should be visually appealing and aligned with your brand messaging. Since you are targeting users who have engaged with your Instagram profile, consider using images or videos related to your Instagram content.
    • Choose your ad placement. The ad placement should be selected as "Instagram" since you are targeting users who have engaged with your Instagram profile.
    • Set your targeting to "Engaged with your business profile on Instagram". This will ensure that your ads are only shown to users who have previously engaged with your Instagram profile.
    • Review and confirm your ad. Before publishing the ad, make sure to review it to ensure that there are no errors.

By following this SOP, you can create retargeting ads specifically for users who have engaged with your Instagram profile. These ads can help you increase engagement and conversions from users who have already shown interest in your brand on Instagram.

Budget and Campaign Structure


When setting up your retargeting campaign, consider the following best practices in terms of budget and campaign structure:


  • Set a budget that is appropriate for your retargeting audience size and campaign goals. A higher budget does not always guarantee better results.
  • Use a single campaign with multiple ad sets to target different segments of your retargeting audience. Targeting different segments can help you personalize your ad messaging and optimize your ad spend.
  • Set different ad creatives for each ad set to test which ones perform the best. Testing different ad creatives can help you identify the best-performing ads and optimize your ad spend.
