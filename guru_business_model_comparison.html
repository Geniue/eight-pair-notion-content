Guru Business Model Comparison



First of all thanks for showing interest in learning the next steps, momentum is key and you’ve already started gaining it.


They are all very similar, and you need to understand all of them.


Now when starting your own business you need to know that all of these businesses require marketing efforts, so from here you need to choose the product you can supply to your marketing efforts with the highest ROI for the most efficient in time, energy, and capital.


The business model that I personally go for every time is building an E-commerce brand based off of a spike in demand I find from seeing other Successful companies arise or finding a trend with a software like explodingtopics.com.


From there I look at all of our available product types below plus more that I dint list like digital products, SAAS etc… and then figure out which one suits my market the best, which one is the easiest and quickest to get started (some can be started instantly), which one takes the least capital risk, and which one takes the least fulfilment effort so I can scale without working about growing a team, or buying more inventory so I can focus all my efforts on growing the business instead of maintaining the business.



The businesses I start hire SMMA Agencies to delegate my marketing, sell on amazon as an extra source of revenue, hire affiliate marketers to sell our product for us, etc…



I hope this made it easy for you to understand to look more at the bigger picture and allow yourself to float on top of all these gurus information and choose the best opportunity for yourself to hit your financial, and lifestyle goals.



SMMA


Pros:

  • Low overhead costs

  • Ability to work remotely


Cons:

  • Requires constant networking and business development

  • Requires hiring and managing a team to scale up

  • Very similar to owning a landscaping business, or car detailing business through zoom meetings

  • Taking never ending sales calls
    • Difficulty to delegate sales calls

  • Dealing with constant client requests



Amazon FBA


Pros:

  • Simple to start and understand

  • Amazon handles shipping and customer service


Cons:

  • Highly competitive market

  • Not much flexibility with scaling through marketing/advertising

  • investment in inventory ($3000+ for testing 1-2 products)

  • Difficult to build of reputation



Amazon Wholesale


Pros:

  • Ability to sell established brands that already are proven to sell

  • Amazon handles shipping and customer service

  • Often Lower profit margins


Cons:

  • Requires significant investment in inventory

  • Requires a relationship with established brands

  • Requires on the ground labour or moving inventory from warehouse to warehouse

  • Especially in early stages of your business, you’ll need to stay close to your warehouses. Cant work completely remote.



Shopify Dropshipping


Pros:

  • Very Low initial investment (can be as low as $100)

  • Ability to scale up almost instantly
  • Easy to start and run
  • No shipping or fulfillment. Suppliers handle it


Cons:

  • Highly competitive market

  • Limited control over branding inventory



Shopify Print on Demand


Pros:

  • No need for inventory

  • Low overhead costs
  • Ability to offer a wide range of products


Cons:

  • Limited profit margins but can be made up by scaleability and margins will expand once product is proven to sell and you switch to a private supplier.



Affiliate Marketing


Pros:

  • No need to create a product

  • Ability to earn passive income
  • Low overhead costs


Cons:

  • Limited control over the product or service being promoted

  • Highly competitive market

  • Very difficult to setup ads without a pixel

  • Run into many issues with ad platform suspensions
